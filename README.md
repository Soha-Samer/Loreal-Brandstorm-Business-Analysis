## 📌 Project Overview
I participated in the L’Oréal Brandstorm 2026 global competition as a **Business Analyst**. My goal was to take a complex business brief and find a data-backed way to innovate for the L'Oréal Luxe fragrance division. 

## 📊 Simple Data Insights
We didn't just guess what young shoppers wanted; we looked at the numbers:
* **64%** of young people are willing to pay more for scents that boost their mood.
* **83%** are open to trying AI-driven fragrance recommendations.
* **45%** of luxury purchases are now influenced by social media and peer reviews.

![Brandstorm Insights](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Soha-Samer/Loreal-Brandstorm-Business-Analysis/main/Brandstorm_Insights.png)

## 👥 The People We Designed For
To make our strategy real, I created three customer "personas" based on global trends:
* **Aria (22, NYC):** A digital native who picks scents based on her "mood boards" and playlist energy.
* **Lena (30, London):** A professional who values sustainability, clean ingredients, and ethical luxury.
* **Jade (28, Seoul):** A social trendsetter who discovers new products through TikTok and viral collaborations.

![Brandstorm Personas](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Soha-Samer/Loreal-Brandstorm-Business-Analysis/main/Brandstorm_Personas.png)

## 🧠 The "Neuro-Tech" Concept
We proposed a smart recommendation engine that uses data to suggest perfumes that actually help people feel better:
* **Bergamot:** Proven to reduce stress levels by up to 23%.
* **Lavender:** Clinically shown to improve sleep quality by 45%.

![Brandstorm Tech & Feasibility](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Soha-Samer/Loreal-Brandstorm-Business-Analysis/main/Brandstorm_Tech_Feasibility.png)

## 🛠️ The Strategic Takeaway
This project shows how I combine **Technical Rigor** (from my Computer Science background) with **Strategic Empathy**. 

> "Data tells you what happened; Empathy tells you what to do next."
